What is this project for?

The advent of Short Message Service (SMS) technology has greatly shifted the nature of communication. The ease and accessibility has led to the rise in spam texts. Bad actors are taking advantage of other people through malicous use of this technology.

SMS Relevancy

The rise of instant messaging (IM) has not eliminated SMS technology due mobile data limitations. Many people in the Philippines still rely on SMS regularly.

Republic Act No. 11934

Also known as the Subscriber Identity Module (SIM) Registration Act, was approved on 10 Oct 2022 with the primary purpose of is eliminating malicious usage of SMS technology.

Preventive Measures

Technology and society is ever-changing, hence, a static solution is a band-aid solution at best. Machine models may allow an expandable framework for filtering spam texts.

This project aims to study the spam messages and extract data that could be used to develop an additional and effective filter against them.

Main Question

What could be the trends and patterns of spam messages in the Philippines?


Secondary Question

Is there a difference between Spam messages before and after the deadline of the Sim Card Registration Act?
